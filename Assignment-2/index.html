If you're considering moving to East Asia but want to maintain a high quality of life with a low cost of living, Malaysia is a compelling option. As a diverse and multicultural nation, Malaysia offers a unique blend of modern infrastructure, vibrant cultures, and rich traditions, making it an attractive destination for expats and digital nomads alike. English is widely spoken, making it accessible for international residents, and the country boasts delicious food, tropical weather, and a mix of urban and nature-filled environments. Whether you're drawn to the dynamic city life of Kuala Lumpur or the serene beaches of Penang, Malaysia has something for everyone, and it remains one of the most affordable places to live in the region.

Cost of Living vs Purchasing Power Index (2022)

The scatter plot shows countries where purchasing power exceeds the cost of living, indicated by those below the dashed line. Malaysia stands out with low living costs and solid purchasing power, offering an affordable yet balanced lifestyle. Other notable options include Singapore and Japan, which also offer a favorable ratio for those interested in higher purchasing power but at a higher living cost.

Quality of Life Indexes (2022)

Malaysia offers a good quality of life, with lower living costs and a solid Purchasing Power Index. Although its Quality of Life Index (118) ranks below Japan and Singapore, it excels in housing affordability with a Property Price to Income Ratio of 9.8, much better than its peers. Malaysia's Health Care Index (70.1) is also respectable, and its commute times and pollution levels are comparable to other East Asian countries. Overall, Malaysia strikes a balance between affordability and quality of living.

For more details on "Quality of Life" indexes, visit Numbeo.

Human Development Index by State

The Malaysia Human Development Index (MHDI) measures key dimensions of human development, including health, education, and standard of living, providing insight into overall well-being across states. Higher MHDI scores indicate regions with better access to healthcare, education, and economic opportunities. Therefore, the MHDI is an important indicator to consider when deciding where to live in Malaysia.

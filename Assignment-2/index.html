If you're considering moving to East Asia but want to maintain a high quality of life with a low cost of living, Malaysia is a compelling option. As a diverse and multicultural nation, Malaysia offers a unique blend of modern infrastructure, vibrant cultures, and rich traditions, making it an attractive destination for expats and digital nomads alike. English is widely spoken, making it accessible for international residents, and the country boasts delicious food, tropical weather, and a balance of urban and natural environments. Whether you're drawn to the dynamic city life of Kuala Lumpur or the serene beaches of Penang, Malaysia has something for everyone, remaining one of the most affordable places to live in the region.

Cost of Living vs Purchasing Power Index (2022)

This bubble plot shows countries where purchasing power exceeds the cost of living, indicated by those below the dashed line. Malaysia stands out with low living costs and solid purchasing power, offering an affordable yet balanced lifestyle. Other notable options include Singapore and Japan, which also offer a favorable ratio for those seeking higher purchasing power, though at a higher living cost.

For more details on "Cost of Living" indexes, visit Numbeo.

Quality of Life Indexes (2022)

Malaysia offers a good quality of life with lower living costs and a solid Purchasing Power Index. Although its Quality of Life Index (118) ranks below Japan and Singapore, it excels in housing affordability with a Property Price to Income Ratio of 9.8, far better than its peers. Malaysia's Health Care Index (70.1) is also respectable, and its commute times and pollution levels are comparable to other East Asian countries. Overall, Malaysia strikes a balance between affordability and quality of life.

For more details on "Quality of Life" indexes, visit Numbeo.

Average Net Income by State (2022)

This map highlights the differences in household income and expenditure across Malaysian states, identifying regions where residents are left with more disposable income after expenses. Kuala Lumpur and Selangor lead with the highest net incomes, reflecting stronger economic opportunities, financial stability, and a better quality of life.

Consumer Price Index of Key States

This chart highlights the cost of living in key Malaysian states, showing how the prices of goods and services impact daily life. Selangor, Johor, and Penang stand out with both high net incomes and higher CPIs, indicating that while residents earn more, they also face higher living costs. In contrast, Sabah offers a unique balance with a relatively high net income and the lowest CPI, making it an attractive option for those seeking affordability without compromising financial stability.

For more details on Consumer Price Index, visit DOSM.

Human Development Index of Key States

The Malaysia Human Development Index (MHDI) measures key aspects of human development, including health, education, and standard of living, providing insight into well-being across states. Higher MHDI scores indicate better access to healthcare, education, and economic opportunities, making this an important factor to consider when deciding where to live in Malaysia.


For more details on Human Development Index, visit DOSM.

Population Density by State (1980-2024)

The map illustrates how densely populated each Malaysian state has become over time. Population density plays a key role in choosing a place to live, as it reflects the level of urbanization. Some may prefer the hustle and bustle of highly dense areas like Kuala Lumpur or Penang, while others might favour the quieter, more peaceful atmosphere of less dense states like Pahang or Sarawak.
